Overview

Worship is central to Emmanuel's identity.

This ministry is not only about singing. It is about leading the church to respond to God with humility, skill, and confidence.

Worship Arts Ministry keeps the gathered church focused on Jesus through praise teams, production support, and seasonal choir moments that strengthen Sunday worship.

The work is shaped by reverence, excellence, teamwork, and discipleship. Those values are what make the ministry feel more like formation than performance.

The team is led by Worship Arts Pastor Laura Ediger, with Pastor Marc Riegel available for more information at (785) 263-3342.

Core commitments

Four priorities shape the way the team leads worship.

These are the values that keep the ministry centered, disciplined, and useful to the gathered church.

Reverence

Lead with awe, not performance.

The team exists to help the church respond to God with humility, gratitude, and clarity.

Excellence

Prepare well and serve with care.

Musical and technical leaders give their best so gathered worship stays focused and distraction-free.

Teamwork

Voices, instruments, and tech serve as one body.

Every role matters because worship arts is built around collaboration and shared responsibility.

Formation

Every service shapes discipleship.

Rehearsals, planning, and service all form people as worshipers before they ever step on stage.

Serving lanes

The live page keeps the ministry structure simple.

There are three main ways to get involved: praise teams, tech teams, and seasonal choirs.

Praise teams

Lead the church in Sunday worship.

Singers and musicians help guide the congregation through adult and youth worship services.

Tech teams

Support sound, screens, lighting, and streaming.

Behind-the-scenes volunteers shape the environment so worship stays clear and attentive.

Seasonal choirs

Lift the room during key church moments.

Choirs gather around the calendar for special Sundays and seasonal worship emphasis.

How to join

Take the next step if you want to sing, play, or serve behind the scenes.

The path is straightforward: reach out, talk through your gifts, train well, and serve consistently.

Step 1

Reach out to the ministry team.

Start with Pastor Marc or the church office so someone can help you find the right place to serve.

Step 2

Talk through your gifts and schedule.

The team will help you understand the expectations for singers, musicians, or production volunteers.

Step 3

Join rehearsal or training.

Worship teams stay sharp through preparation, prayer, and a willingness to learn together.

Step 4

Serve consistently and keep growing.

The goal is not just filling a slot. The goal is forming leaders who help the church worship well.
